What You Need for Your Homeschool Evaluation

Here are the requirements and recommendations to prepare for your Pennsylvania homeschool evaluation.

Portfolio Samples

Include 2 to 3 samples for each required subject, showing work from the beginning, middle, and end of the year. You may bring entire workbooks or pages. Examples include copy work, worksheets, artwork, science experiments, tests, creative writing, and projects that represent your child’s learning.

Partial Year Samples

If your child began the year in another setting, include classwork from that time to establish a baseline and show progress during the homeschool period.

Required Subjects

Elementary (Grades 1–6)

  • English, including spelling, reading, and writing
  • Arithmetic
  • Science
  • Social studies, including geography, U.S. and PA history, and civics
  • Safety education, with regular instruction on fire prevention
  • Health and physiology
  • Physical education
  • Music
  • Art

Secondary (Grades 7–12)

  • English, including literature, speech, and composition
  • Science (biology, chemistry)
  • Social studies, including world history, economics, geography, and civics
  • Math, including general math, algebra, and geometry (trigonometry recommended)
  • Art
  • Music
  • Physical education, health, and safety education
  • Foreign language (recommended but not required)

Reading Log

Maintain a list of books read or read aloud, including audiobooks. Younger students often log 30+ books per year, older students may log fewer depending on reading level. You may use the provided template or your own format.

Standardized Assessment Results

For grades 3, 5, and 8, include the results of a nationally normed test (e.g. California Achievement Test). Scores need not be passing, and tests may be at a lower grade level to match ability.

Hours & Days

Elementary students need 900 hours or 180 days, secondary need 990 hours or 180 days. Most families record days rather than hours. Your statement that you met these requirements is sufficient.

Other Information

Evaluation Deadline

Submit your evaluation form by June 30. If late, the district will contact you before taking action.

Student Must Attend

The student interview is required by law. Your child need not stay for the entire evaluation discussion.

Fee

$40 per student. Payment options: PayPal, Zelle, or Venmo. We will send payment instructions after the evaluation.

Scheduling

Evaluations are scheduled in May and June, up to two weeks in advance. I teach full-time, so early booking is encouraged.

Students with IEPs

If your child had an IEP, you must either revoke the IEP or get objectives pre-approved. I offer free objective development to support families with special needs.

Evaluation Prep Checklist

  • Portfolio samples from each subject
  • Reading log
  • Standardized test results (grades 3, 5, 8)
  • Fee payment arrangements ($40 per child, $25 per sibling)
  • List of any questions you have

Start of Year Forms

  • An Affidavit or Unsworn Declaration
  • Educational objectives (elementary and secondary)
  • Medical and dental reports or exemption form
