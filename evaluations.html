Prepare for Your Homeschool Evaluation

Everything you need to ensure a smooth, compliant process in Pennsylvania

Book Your Evaluation

Portfolio Samples

Include 2–3 samples per subject (beginning, middle, end of year). Bring worksheets, artwork, reports, tests, and projects that showcase your child’s learning journey.

Partial Year Samples

If your child switched to homeschooling mid-year, include early-year schoolwork to establish a baseline and demonstrate progress under your guidance.

Required Subjects

Elementary (Grades 1–6)

  • English (spelling, reading, writing)
  • Arithmetic
  • Science
  • Social Studies (history, geography, civics)
  • Health & Safety Education
  • PE, Music, Art

Secondary (Grades 7–12)

  • English (literature, speech, composition)
  • Science (biology, chemistry)
  • Math (algebra, geometry; trig recommended)
  • Social Studies (economics, world history)
  • PE, Music, Art
  • Foreign Language (recommended)

Reading Log

Document titles and authors of books read or read-alouds. Aim for 30+ entries for younger students; adapt as needed for older readers.

Standardized Assessments

Third, fifth, and eighth graders include nationally normed test results (e.g., CAT). Scores needn’t be passing; tests can match your child’s level.

Hours & Days

Elementary requires 900 hours or 180 days; secondary 990 hours or 180 days. Recording days is sufficient—your signed statement meets legal requirements.

Other Information & Checklist

  • • Portfolio samples (2–3 per subject)
  • • Reading log
  • • Standardized test results (grades 3, 5, 8)
  • • Fee payment arrangements ($40/student; $25 sibling)
  • • List any questions you have

Start-of-Year Forms

Submit an Affidavit, Educational Objectives (K–6 or 7–12), and medical/dental reports or exemption by August 1.
